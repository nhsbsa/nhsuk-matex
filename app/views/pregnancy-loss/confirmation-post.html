What happens next?

We'll post the certificate to the mother. They should receive the certificate within 10 working days.

If the mother has not received her certificate within the given timeframe, ask the mother to contact the NHSBSA XYZ.

We recommend that the mother pays for their prescription items until they receive their paper certificate.

The mother should ask the pharmacist for an FP57 receipt. They could receive a refund if their maternity exemption certificate covered the date that they claimed NHS prescriptions.

Using the certificate

The certificate is valid one month prior to the date that the application was received.

The mother can start using their exemption straight away.

If the certificate covers the date the mother decides to collect their free NHS prescriptions, the patient will not have to pay for their items.

The mother should mark box 'D' on the prescription form and sign the declaration when collecting their items.

The mother may need to show their certificate as evidence that their exemption is valid. They can still collect free NHS prescriptions if they do not have their certificate with them.

How long is the certificate valid for?
When it starts

The certificate is valid one month prior to the date we receive the mother's application.

If the certificate covers the date the mother collects their items, they can get their NHS prescriptions for free.

The mother should mark box 'D' on the prescription form and sign the declaration.

The mother may need to show their certificate as evidence that their exemption is valid. The mother can still collect their NHS prescriptions for free, even if they do not have their certificate with them.

When it expires

The certificate will expire 12 months after either:

  • the mother's due date
  • the birth of their baby

If the baby is born later than expected, the mother should contact us with a copy of the baby's birth certificate. We will extend the expiry date and send the mother a new certificate.

How to claim a refund
NHS prescription charges

The mother may get an NHS prescription charge refund if:

  • they get an NHS refund form (FP57) when they pay - they cannot get one later
  • their maternity exemption certificate covers the date they paid for their NHS prescriptions

The FP57 tells them how to claim.

The mother will need to apply for a refund within three months of paying the prescription charge.

NHS Prescription Prepayment Certificate (PPC) fees

If the mother holds a valid PPC, they may be entitled to a refund. They can read the HC11 booklet (PDF: 284KB) for details of PPC refund arrangements.

How to report a change in details or an error

It is the mother's responsibility to check their personal details on the certificate are correct and are kept up to date.

If they change their name, they'll need a new certificate. The mother should return the certificate to us with a copy of either their:

  • marriage certificate
  • civil partnership certificate
  • deed poll
  • passport
  • decree absolute

If the mother changes address, notices any errors on their certificate, loses or damages their certificate they should seek more information.

Accidentally received more than one certificate

If the mother has received more than one maternity exemption certificate with different certificate numbers, it means we have received more than one application for them.

As their entitlement to these certificates has been authorised, they can use either of these certificates to claim free NHS prescriptions.

Miscarriage or stillbirth

The mother is eligible for a maternity exemption certificate:

  • if they are currently pregnant
  • for 12 months after the birth of a live child
  • if they have had a miscarriage
  • for 12 months after the birth of a child registered as stillborn

The mother can continue to use their certificate for free NHS prescriptions.

The mother is still entitled to free NHS dental treatment if this happened during, or after, their 24th week of pregnancy.

For more information about NHS dental treatment, visit our website.
